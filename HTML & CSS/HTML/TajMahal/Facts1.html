9 Significant facts of Taj Mahal

  • Taj Mahal is located in Agra city which is located in Utar Pradesh region in Northern India.


  • Taj Mahal is located in Agra city which is located in Utar Pradesh region in Northern India.


  • The name 'Taj Mahal' is translated to mean 'Crown of Palaces'. It was named as such to refer to the beauty of the deceased Persian princess and wife to Shah Jahan.


  • Taj Mahal is 171 meters (561 feet) tall.


  • The pillars on the Taj Mahal are tilted outwards to ensure that in case of an earthquake, they will fall away from the tomb.


  • Despite many of the precious stones on the mausoleum being plucked off by the British Army during the Indian War of 1857, the mausoleum is still beautiful.



  • You can see the Taj Mahal from the Jasmine Tower of Agra Fort (also known as Musamman Burj- where Shah Jahan was imprisoned by his son, Aurangzeb).


  • Taj Mahal which is one of the most visited and beautiful monuments in India, has over 4-8 million visitors annually. Sometimes there are over 40-50 thousand visitors


  • The tomb contains 99 different names of Allah as calligraphic inscriptions.


  • Other than Shah Jahan his other wives and maids are also burried outside the Taj Mahal.
